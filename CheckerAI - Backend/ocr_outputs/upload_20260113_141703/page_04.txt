(ii) Rooms provided to in-patient or out patients will be taxable if the room charges exceeds ₹5000/- per day. Only ICU, NICCU, NICU room charges are exempt, how here as charges exceeded the threshold GST is payable.

(iii) Generally, cosmetic surgery, plastic surgery, hair transplantation service provided will not constitute as Medical service. However if such service is provided to restore the body part, for growth development, to fight auto immune diseases, it is medical service. Hence plastic surgery conducted to repair cleft lip of new born baby is Exempt.

(iv) Ambulance services to transport critically ill patient is exempt. However if it is used for other purpose like transport of passengers it becomes taxable.

(v) Food Supply to in-patients is an exempt supply no gst will be payable.

(V) Unani, Siddha, Ayurveda, Homeopathy, Allopathy service comes under Medical services for public health and hence Homeopathic medical treatment is exempt, no GST payable.
